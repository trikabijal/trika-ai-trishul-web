## 2. Vocabulary Table

| Use This | Never This | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| trika.ai (lowercase, with .ai) | Trika.ai, TRIKA.AI | Domain and brand name in running text is always lowercase. |
| Trika (capitalized) | trika, TRIKA | Company name when not using the domain form. "Trika is the company we built..." |
| 18+ years (founders/team only) | "AuditPro has 18+ years..." | The experience claim belongs to the founders. AuditPro is new to the US market. Conflating the two is the central credibility risk. |
| major automotive OEMs | Mahindra, Nissan, Renault, Tata, or any named OEM | No specific OEM names. Ever. Use "major automotive OEMs" or "Tier-1 automotive OEMs." |
| gross per deal | gross per copy | Dealership floor language. |
| your trainer | your 20 Group coach | More universal. Not every dealer uses a 20 Group. (Note: the brief uses "20 Group coach"; the deployed site uses "your trainer." Keep "your trainer.") |
| multi-point inspection | MPI | Spell it out for clarity. |
| sits alongside / layers on top of | replaces, competes with | AuditPro is additive to the dealer's existing stack, never a replacement. |
| CDK, VinSolutions, DealerSocket, Reynolds & Reynolds | Any claim of "integration" or "partnership" | Name them as co-existing tools. Never claim formal partnerships unless they exist. |
| accountability | AI-powered intelligence, AI-driven insights | AI is the implementation detail. Accountability is the hero. |
| audit, verify, measure | empower, transform, reimagine | Use operational verbs. No SaaS marketing fog. |
| the first product we're bringing to the US | battle-tested, proven across hundreds of dealers | AuditPro is new in the US. The founders are not. |
| Teena Shah | Tina, Tina Shah | Canonical name per LinkedIn and deployed website. |
| AuditPro | Audit Pro, auditpro, AUDITPRO | One word, capital A, capital P. |
| Road to the Sale | Road to Sale, road to the sale, RTTS | Full name, title case. Acceptable shorthand: "Road to Sale" in casual contexts. |
| NADA | Nada, nada | Always all-caps. It is an acronym. |
| F&I | F and I, f&i, FNI | Ampersand, both caps. |
| T.O. (turn-over) | TO, turnover | Periods, both caps. Spell out on first use. |

### Banned words (never use in any channel)

empower, transform, reimagine, unlock, supercharge, next-generation, revolutionary, seamless, best-in-class, holistic, synergy, end-to-end, leverage (as a verb), battle-tested, proven at scale, trusted by industry leaders

## 3. Rules Checklist

Quick-check version of the brief's global rules. Scan before publishing anything.

**Experience claims**
- "18+ years" applies to founders/team only, never to AuditPro itself
- Never say "AuditPro has been deployed at scale" or "hundreds of dealers"
- Never cite specific dealer counts, revenue figures, or customer counts
- Structure: experience claim --> AuditPro as new US product --> NADA anchor

**OEM and naming**
- No specific OEM names (no Mahindra, Nissan, Renault, Tata, etc.)
- No mention of Tiez except once in Teena's bio on the founders page
- No mention of AnnotatorPro or any unnamed future product
- No "coming soon" pages for products that do not exist

**Product claims**
- AI is an implementation detail; accountability is the hero
- AuditPro "sits alongside" the dealer stack; never "replaces" or "competes with"
- Named integrations (CDK, VinSolutions, etc.) are framed as co-existing, not partnerships
- No pricing anywhere -- website, form, FAQ, or footer

**Content restrictions**
- No testimonials or case studies without founder approval (no fictional quotes)
- No internal GTM content (ICPs, prospect names, deadlines, known limitations)
- No stock imagery (handshakes, skylines, smiling diverse teams)
- No generic SaaS framing ("Three reasons to choose Trika," "Loved by sales teams")
- Industry stats (Brooks Group 95%/69%, HBR 28%, test drive 10-15%) require founder verification before publishing

**Voice**
- Short sentences. Real numbers. Named processes.
- Use dealership floor language (deals, desking, Tower, walkaround, first pencil, F&I handoff)
- No SaaS-corporate vocabulary (see banned words list above)
- Confident, not salesy. Specific, not generic.
